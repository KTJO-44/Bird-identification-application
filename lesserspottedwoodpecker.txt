GENERAL: Smallest of the
woodpeckers. Also the least
strikingly patterned, its barring
somewhat blurred and diffuse. It
spends much of its time in
higher, more slender branches.
NESTING: Hole in tree,
3cm in diameter.
FEEDING: Chips out insects
and their larvae from beneath
loose or rotten bark; also takes
insects from thick, woody plant
stems close to ground.
