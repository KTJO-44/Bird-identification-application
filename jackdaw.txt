GENERAL: A small Crow, the
Jackdaw is an excellent flier,
and flocks frequently soar and
glide over woods or around cliffs
and quarries.
NESTING: Pile of sticks lined
with animal dung, mud, roots,
moss and hair, in hole in tree or
building, or old chimney.
FEEDING: Forages on ground,
taking earthworms, seeds and
scraps; also takes caterpillars
from foliage, and berries.
