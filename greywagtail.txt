GENERAL: Slimmest of the
wagtails, and with the longest
tail. Often inhabits the areas of
clear, fast-flowing, rocky streams
in summer and shallow pools in
winter. May be confused with the
Yellow Wagtail.
NESTING: Grassy cup in
hole in bank, wall, tree roots,
or under bridge.
FEEDING: Catches flies and
small invertebrates on ground or
in air; quite active; bouncy feeder.
