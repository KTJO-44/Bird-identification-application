GENERAL: Clings to branches
of tall trees, forages on rocks,
walls and old buildings and quite
often visits the ground. It
also comes to feeders in gardens
near woods.
NESTING: Uses old woodpecker
hole or nest box, lined with bark
and leaves, typically plastering
mud around entrance.
FEEDING: Seeds, berries, nuts,
acorns, and beech-mast; visits
peanut baskets.
