GENERAL: One of Europe's
smallest birds, the Wren uses
a remarkable variety of habitats.
It spends most of the time low
down, or near the ground, often in
deep thickets of bramble, bracken
or ornamental shrubberies.
NESTING: Small, loose ball of
leaves and grass, in bank.
FEEDING: Finds insects and
spiders and feeds on scraps
scattered under bushes.
