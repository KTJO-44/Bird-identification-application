GENERAL: A woodland bird that
also ventures into parks and large
gardens, the Jay is usually shy and
not easy to watch for long periods.
NESTING: Bulky nest of sticks,
low down in thick bush.
FEEDING: Eats anything from
caterpillars to small rodents;
chiefly insects in summer with
some eggs and small chicks and
acorns (collected and stored)
in winter.
