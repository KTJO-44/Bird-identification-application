GENERAL: A striking, black and
white social bird, usually seen
in pairs but often form small groups
and occasionally fill whole trees
with flocks of 20-40 at a time,
when gathering to roost.
NESTING: Big, domed nest of
sticks with solid mud inner core,
lined with roots and scraps.
FEEDING: Insects, grain and
scraps in a range of habitats, from
fields to car parks and roadsides;
eats eggs and chicks in summer.
