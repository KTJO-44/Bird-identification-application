GENERAL: A typical forest or
woodland-edge species, adapted 
to follow foraging animals such 
as a wild boar: it picks up food 
from the earth  overturned by the
animals.
NESTING: Domed nest of leaves
and grass in bank, dense bush
or hedge, or thick ivy.
FEEDING: Mostly on ground,
hopping and flitting in search
of insects, berries, seeds.
