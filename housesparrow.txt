GENERAL: Familiar in towns
and gardens and even in farmland
concentrate around buildings.
Males are easily recognised.
Females lack the bold patterns.
NESTING: Untidy nest of grass
and feathers, in roof space,
cavity in wall, in House Martin
nest, tree cavity or more openly
in creepers and around wires.
FEEDING: Seeds, buds, roots,
berries, insects for young.
Visits bird-tables.
