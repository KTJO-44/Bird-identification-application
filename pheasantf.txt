GENERAL: 'Wild' pheasants
often resort to marshy, reedy
places, as well as woodland edges
where they are most familiar and
characteristic.
NESTING: Hollow on ground,
under overhanging cover such as
brambles, unlined or thin
scatterings of grass stems.
FEEDING: Takes all kind
of food, from seeds and berries
to insects and lizards, from
ground in its powerful bill.
