GENERAL: Kestrels are the
pigeon-sized, long-winged, daytime
hunter most often seen perched on
telegraph poles or wires or
hovering over roadsides, as if
suspended on a string.
NESTING: Bare ledges on cliffs,
in quarries, derelict buildings,
high window-ledges, disused
crows' nests or tree holes.
FEEDING: Catches small
mammals, beetles, lizards,
earthworms and small birds.
