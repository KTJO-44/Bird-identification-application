GENERAL: One of Europe's
smallest birds, it is everywhere
associated with conifers. It is
also a garden bird in many places.
Coal Tits typically exploit their
weight by searching the thinnest
twigs.
NESTING: Hair-lined small cup
of moss and leaves in hole in
stump, tree, wall or ground, in
small-holed nest box.
FEEDING: Tiny insects, seeds and
nuts, visiting feeders.
