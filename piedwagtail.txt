GENERAL: Frequently seen on
roofs, from which it typically calls
before moving off; a useful
indicator of its presence. In
summer, it can be found anywhere
from builders' yards, woodsheds,
remote quarries and natural cliffs,
along stony river or lake sides.
NESTING: Grassy cup in cavity in
bank, cliff, woodpile, under bridge.
FEEDING: Flying in pursuit
of flies; takes insects,
molluscs, and some seeds.
