GENERAL: A small, social thrush,
easily identified by its well-
marked head pattern. It moves
about in flocks, often mixed with
Fieldfares. In winter, it feeds in
loose congregations in fields or in
closer groups, feeding on berries
in hedgerows.
NESTING: Cup of grass and
twigs, in low bush or shrub.
FEEDING: Worms, insects and
seeds; feeds on berries; visits
gardens for apples and berries.
