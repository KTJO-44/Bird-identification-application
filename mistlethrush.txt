GENERAL: A large, bold,
aggressive thrush, the Mistle
Thrush is the largest of the
'spotted' thrushes. It is usually
found in pairs, but families join
up in larger groups during autumn
when berries are abundant.
NESTING: Big, loose cup of roots,
leaves, twigs and grass, often
quite exposed high on branch.
FEEDING: Hops on ground,
searching for worms, seeds,
invertebrates; eats berries; fruit.
